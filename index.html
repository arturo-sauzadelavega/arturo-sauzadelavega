Poly

Polymers

Plastic pollution is a major environmental concern that requires immediate action. One potential solution is to replace petroleum-based plastics with biodegradable or compostable polymers made from biomass-derived monomers. Our research aims to better understand the mechanisms of ring-opening polymerization (ROP) and identify ways to optimize the process through the development of faster and more efficient catalysts.

PVK

Perovskites

Metal-halide perovskites (MHPs) are a class of materials that have gained significant attention due to their exceptional performance in solar cells and light-emitting diodes. Our research focuses on improving the efficiency of exciton generation in bulk perovskite materials and enhancing charge transport between perovskite nanocrystals.

Perovskites Nanocrystal 2D Layered PVK

Image
PCET

Proton-Coupled Electron Transfer

Small-molecule mononuclear copper(III) compounds which mimic biological molecules/enzymes have been found to initiate hydrocarbon oxidation by activating X–H bonds. These reactions can occur through hydrogen-atom transfer (HAT) or concerted proton-coupling electron transfer (cPCET). We utilize theoretical techniques to distinguish between HAT and cPCET mechanisms.

PCET HAT Copper(III)

MOF

Metal-Organic Frameworks

Metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) are porous crystalline materials with exceptional surface areas and tunable properties. Our research explores enzyme-mimicking catalysis by MOFs and the post-synthetic modification of MOFs to introduce active catalytic sites for applications in hydrogenation and C–H activation reactions.
